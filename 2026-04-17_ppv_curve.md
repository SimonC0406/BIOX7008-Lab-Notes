## Overview

Generated Positive Predictive Value (PPV) curve as a function of MAX_DS threshold.
PPV quantifies SpliceAI's real-world predictive accuracy using RNA-seq confirmation as ground truth.

---

## Tasks Completed

### 1. PPV Analysis

**Definition:**
```
PPV(t) = |Step1 ∩ Step2 ∩ MAX_DS ≥ t|  /  |Step1 ∩ MAX_DS ≥ t|
```

Where:
- Step 1: Any_splicing_aberration = YES (2,326 variants)
- Step 2: RNA-seq expression-confirmed (pileup intersection, ~1,001 variants)

**Result:** `ppv_curve.png`

Key observations from the curve:
- PPV increases monotonically with MAX_DS threshold
- At MAX_DS = 0.0: PPV ≈ 0.43 (43% of Step 1 variants confirmed by RNA-seq)
- At MAX_DS = 0.5: PPV ≈ ~0.6–0.7 (significant enrichment)
- At MAX_DS = 0.9+: PPV approaches 1.0 (high-confidence predictions)

### 2. Threshold Trade-off

| MAX_DS | Approx. PPV | Variant Count (two-step) |
|--------|-------------|--------------------------|
| 0.0 | ~0.43 | ~1,001 |
| 0.1 | — | — |
| 0.5 | ~0.65 | ~90 |
| 0.9 | ~0.95 | ~20 |

This validates the two-step strategy: accepting lower MAX_DS but requiring RNA-seq confirmation maintains reasonable PPV while recovering more candidates.
